The B60 Baggage Car

For my current project, I have been working on a Pennsylvania Railroad (PRR) B60 Baggage car. The B60 baggage car first appeared in the early 1900's. 360 were built and they weighed about 103,000 pounds. By the early 1950s, two hundred express messenger cars were in service on the PRR.

I started the project in early October of 2024 and plan to complete it over Fall Break of 2024. I found the files online by user Kaiw on the Cults3d, a website for sharing and selling 3d printing files. The main shell consists of 4 parts while the roof is made up of 6 parts. The body parts took around 24 hours each to complete. I have assembled the car and glues the necessary parts together, but not fully as I still need to cut a piece of wood for the shell to sit on. On most models I build, I like 3d print as much as possible. Once this car is finished, everything except the paint and the wheels will have been 3d printed. I plan to paint this car a Tuscan Red as that is what the PRR used on most of their passenger cars.
